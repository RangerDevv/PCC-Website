OUR STORY

WHAT IS THE Pitt Charity Cup?

The Pitt Charity Cup is a U18 charity cricket tournament held in Pittsburgh, Pennsylvania. Founded and organized entirely by high school students, the tournament brings together young cricketers from across Western PA for a weekend of competitive matches, community building, and fundraising. Every dollar raised goes toward providing cricket equipment to kids who want to play but don't have the means. We started small, but our goal is big — make cricket accessible to every young athlete in the region.

WHY WE Started This

We grew up playing cricket in backyards, parking lots, and whatever open space we could find. Along the way, we met dozens of kids who loved the game just as much as we did but couldn't afford their own gear. That stuck with us. When we realized no one in Pittsburgh was doing anything about it, we decided to step up ourselves. The Pitt Charity Cup wasn't born out of a business plan — it came from seeing friends tape up old bats because they couldn't get new ones. We figured if we could organize a tournament, raise some money, and put real equipment in kids' hands, that would be worth every hour of planning.

WHERE YOUR Money Goes

Cricket Equipment

Providing bats, balls, pads, gloves, and stumps for young players who cannot afford their own gear. We source quality equipment and distribute it directly to kids in the Pittsburgh area.

Venue & Tournament Operations

Covering field rentals, safety equipment, match supplies, and everything needed to run a well-organized and competitive tournament weekend.

Youth Cricket Opportunities

Creating meaningful opportunities for young players to compete, gain match experience, and develop their skills through an organized U18 tournament.

Growing the Local Cricket Community

Bringing together players, students, families, and supporters to strengthen and expand the youth cricket community across Western Pennsylvania.
